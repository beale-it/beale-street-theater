About Page (BSTmini-about.pdf)
BEALE STREET THEATER For over a decade, Beale Street Theater has been Kingman's premier destination for live performance. Located on historic Route 66, we are committed to bringing world-class entertainment to Northern Arizona while fostering a vibrant arts community.
Our Mission: What We Stand For
* Community First: We create opportunities for local artists and provide accessible arts education to youth throughout Mohave County.
* Excellence: From Broadway classics to original works, we maintain the highest standards in every production we present.
* Inclusion: Theater is for everyone. We champion diverse voices and stories that reflect our community's rich tapestry.
* Preservation: Our historic venue on Route 66 honors Kingman's heritage while serving as a cultural anchor for future generations.
Executive Staff: Meet Our Leaders
Our Story: A Decade of Performance Founded in 2016, Beale Street Theater emerged from a simple vision: to create a professional theater company that would serve as the cultural heartbeat of Kingman and Northern Arizona. What began in a converted storefront on historic Route 66 has grown into a thriving arts organization presenting over 30 productions annually.
Our commitment to artistic excellence, community engagement, and youth education has made BST a cornerstone of Mohave County's cultural landscape. Through mainstage productions, youth theater programs, and educational outreach, we've touched the lives of thousands of artists and audience members.
As we celebrate our 10th anniversary in 2026, we look forward to the next chapter of storytelling, community building, and artistic innovation. Thank you for being part of our journey.

5. ABOUT
Beale Street Theater (History & Mission) A CENTURY OF STORYTELLING IN KINGMAN For over a decade, Beale Street Theater has been Kingman's premier destination for live performance. Located on historic Route 66, we are committed to bringing world-class entertainment to Northern Arizona.
Our Mission: To establish and enrich the culture and community of Kingman, AZ, through Art-related education, performances, and venues. We focus on community, creativity, excellence, personal growth, and inclusivity.
Our History: The theatrical landscape in Kingman evolved through four distinct architectural phases, culminating at our current historic site at 3rd and Beale—originally the State Theater built in 1948. After serving as a cinema, Praise Chapel, and Boston Antiques, the building was acquired in 2018 by the Kingman Center for the Arts. Following an intensive 7-year renovation partnered with Axiom Construction, Angle Homes, and T.R. Orr Construction, the theater celebrated its grand reopening as a state-of-the-art performing arts venue.
