A CENTURY OF STORYTELLING

For over a century, this site has functioned as the cultural heart of Kingman, evolving through various forms to meet the changing needs of its citizens. From its earliest days as a multi-purpose lodge to its current incarnation as a state-of-the-art performing arts venue, Beale Street Theater represents the enduring power of community and creativity.

"As you step across this threshold, whether as a student of the arts or a dedicated volunteer, you are not merely entering a building—you are becoming a steward of Kingman's architectural heritage."

Historical Timeline

1920s - 1970s

The Movie Palace Era

The theater served as Kingman's premier cinema, showing films and hosting community gatherings. Known as the State Theater, it became a cornerstone of downtown entertainment.

1979

The Movie Era Ends

After decades as a cinema, the theater played its final film and closed its doors, marking the end of its first life as a movie palace.

1990s

The Spiritual Chapter

The building became Praise Chapel, serving as a house of worship and maintaining its status as a place of community gathering.

2000s

The Commercial Chapter

Transitioning to Boston Antiques thrift store, the space maintained commercial use, keeping the building active and preventing decay.

2017

The Rebirth

Nate and Sara Peterson purchased the building, officially naming it Beale Street Theater and reclaiming its cultural heritage.

2018

KCA Acquisition

Kingman Center for the Arts acquired the property, partnering with Axiom Construction to begin renovation and restoration.

2019 - 2022

Major Construction

With support from Angle Homes, architect Michael Taylor, and T.R. Orr Construction, the venue underwent complete modernization while respecting its historic character.

2025

The Grand Reopening

After seven years of focused effort, Beale Street Theater opened its doors as a fully-realized performing arts venue, ready to serve Kingman for the next century.
