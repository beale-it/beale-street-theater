About Beale Street Theater

Where Kingman’s history meets its future.

Built in 1939 and reborn today, the Beale Street Theater is a hub for arts, entertainment, and education. We believe in the power of live performance to bring people together right here on Historic Route 66.

Our Mission

To create a home for the arts in Kingman.

We are dedicated to enriching the community by providing high-quality theatrical productions, live music, and comprehensive educational programs. By offering a safe and creative space for artists of all ages, we ensure that the performing arts continue to thrive in Mohave County.

Our Vision

  • ✓ Rehabilitate our historic downtown venue into a state-of-the-art facility.
  • ✓ Foster local talent through youth theater and masterclass workshops.
  • ✓ Drive economic revitalization along Historic Route 66.

Our History

From Cinema to Center Stage

The Beale Street Theater has been a cornerstone of Kingman’s downtown since the golden age of cinema. Originally serving as a premier movie house for locals and travelers passing through Route 66, the theater holds decades of memories within its walls.

Purchased and managed by the Kingman Center for the Arts, the building has undergone extensive renovations to transform it into a world-class performing arts venue.

Today, equipped with state-of-the-art sound-dampening panels and lighting, the theater stands ready to host live concerts, community plays, and youth educational programs for the next generation.
