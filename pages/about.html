About Beale Street Theater

Where Kingman’s history meets its future.

Built in 1939 and reborn today, the Beale Street Theater is a hub for arts, entertainment, and education. We believe in the power of live performance to bring people together right here on Historic Route 66.

Our Mission

To create a home for the arts in Kingman.

We are dedicated to enriching the community by providing high-quality theatrical productions, live music, and comprehensive educational programs. By offering a safe and creative space for artists of all ages, we ensure that the performing arts continue to thrive in Mohave County.

Our Vision

  • ✓ Rehabilitate our historic downtown venue into a state-of-the-art facility.
  • ✓ Foster local talent through youth theater and masterclass workshops.
  • ✓ Drive economic revitalization along Historic Route 66.

The Evolution of Beale Street Theater

A Journey Through Time

1979: The Movie Era Ends

After decades of serving as a cinema, the theater played its final film and shuttered its doors, marking the end of its first life as a movie palace.

1990s: The Spiritual Chapter

The building was granted a second life as Praise Chapel, serving as a house of worship and maintaining its status as a place of community gathering.

2000s: The Commercial Chapter

The space transitioned into Boston Antiques, a thrift store that utilized the vast theater floor for local commerce, keeping the roof intact and the doors open.

2017: The Rebirth

The modern era began when Nate and Sara Peterson purchased the building, reclaiming its cultural heritage by officially naming it the Beale Street Theater.

2018: Acquisition

KCA purchased the theater and immediately partnered with Axiom Construction to begin the rigorous process of demolition and site preparation.

2019: Design & Funding

The project reached a major milestone when Angle Homes provided its first matching grant. This funded a collaboration with architect Michael Taylor to design the modern venue.

2022: Major Construction

Under the leadership of T.R. Orr Construction, the project entered its most intensive phase, installing modern HVAC, theater lighting, and house seating.

2025: The Grand Reopening

After seven years of focused effort, the Beale Street Theater opened its brand-new doors, fully realized as a world-class cornerstone for the performing arts.
